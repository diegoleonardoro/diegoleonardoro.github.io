Landmark's Century Centre Cinema

The theatre has been located in 2828 North Clark Street at Diversey since 2001. It is Chicago's first all-stadium seating theatre showcasing independent and foreign language films. Surrounded by award-winning restaurants. It attracts an audience that appreciates good food and art.

Context:

Since its opening in 2001, Landmark's Century Centre Cinema has sucessfully catered to an audience that appeciates foreign, independent and indie movies. Due to its success, in the Spring of 2015, the theatre was extensively renovated, and in 2021, as society moves from the global pandemic, they are looking to open a new location in Chicago.

Challenge:

Chicago has a good supply of theatres that satisfy the demand for independent and foreing movies. They are scattered throughout they city, and they serve a unique audience. Due to one and a half successful decade, with a solid and growing customer base, Landmark's Theatre is now looking for an untapped location to open a new theatre.

To do so, it is necessary to understand the area where Landmark's Century Centre Cinema is located to see if it has unique characteristics that should be replicated in its new location.

Understanding the area where Landmark's Century Centre Cinema is located:

Exploring ridership of the nearest L train station to Landmark's Century Centre Cinema, could help us discover patterns of transit in this area and ultimately decide if the new location could replicate these patterns.

The nearest station to the theatre is Diversey. From 2018 to 2019, Diversey's daliy ridership divided by day of the week looked like this (hover over the bars for more detail):

Rides

The busiest day at Diversey was Tuesdays with 592.345 rides and the least busy day was Sundays with 205.548 riders. The difference between the busiest day and the least busy day was 386.797 rides. That seems like a big difference, but comparing this value to other stations will help us determine if ridership at Diversey is similar to that of other stations.
